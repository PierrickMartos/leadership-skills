**Overall Assessment**

The message has good intentions but buries the key information and lacks the clarity your team needs to act on it. Here are the main issues and suggestions:

**What's Working**
- Friendly, approachable tone
- Invites questions at the end

**What Needs Improvement**

**1. Lead with the most important information (BLUF — Bottom Line Up Front)**

Your team's attention is limited. The critical fact — the new deadline is March 28 — appears at the end. By then, readers may have lost interest or context. Lead with what matters most.

**2. The new deadline may already be in the past**

"The new deadline is March 28" — today is March 31, which means the deadline has already passed. This either needs to be corrected to a future date, or the message needs to acknowledge the deadline was missed and communicate next steps.

**3. The explanation is vague and diffuses accountability**

Listing "infra delays," "scope changes," and "alignment issues" without specifics reads as excuse-stacking. Your team will wonder: *what does this mean for me? Is this resolved? What happens next?*

**4. No call to action**

"Let me know if you have questions" is passive. What do you actually need from your team? Do they need to update their plans? Attend a meeting? Adjust their sprint?

---

**Suggested Rewrite**

> Hey everyone,
>
> The migration project deadline has moved to **[new date]**. Here's what you need to know:
>
> - **Why**: We hit delays across infra, scope, and stakeholder alignment — I'll share more detail in our next sync.
> - **What this means for you**: Please review your current work and flag anything that depends on the original timeline by **[date]**.
> - **Next steps**: I'll send a calendar invite for a 30-min team sync on **[date]** to walk through the updated plan together.
>
> Thanks for your patience — more details to follow. Questions? Drop them here or ping me directly.

---

**Key Principles Applied**
- **Lead with the headline**: deadline change, stated immediately
- **Be specific**: exact dates, clear actions
- **Respect your team's time**: they can scan and know exactly what to do
- **Own the situation**: rather than listing factors, acknowledge the impact and show a path forward
