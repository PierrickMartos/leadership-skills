**What changed and why**
- **Moved the deadline to the first line.** In the original, "The new deadline is March 28" is buried near the end after six sentences of context. On Slack, many readers will only see the first line in their notification preview — if it starts with "Hey everyone! Just wanted to give a quick update," they still don't know what the update is about. The deadline is the point; it goes first.
- **Cut the preamble.** "Just wanted to give a quick update," "So we've been working on this for a few months," and "Anyway, the point is..." are all warm-up before the actual message. They add length without adding meaning. On Slack, this signals the wrong medium — if context is genuinely needed, a brief summary first, then thread it or link a doc.
- **Kept the causes brief and secondary.** The reasons for the delay are relevant but they're not the bottom line. Mentioning them in one line after the key fact preserves the information without making the reader work for the headline.
