**What matters**
We have a clear recommendation to migrate our data platform to Snowflake. The analysis is complete: three platforms were evaluated, 14 stakeholder teams were consulted, and the numbers are straightforward — $2.1M over three years versus $3.4M today, a projected saving of $1.3M. The migration plan is defined, phased over 18 months, with a low-risk six-week pilot to start.

**Why now**
Steering committee approval is needed to launch Phase 1. The team is ready to move, and the longer we delay the start, the longer we defer $1.3M in savings and continue accumulating technical debt on the current platform.

**Risk if we do nothing**
Deferring a decision doesn't hold the current spend — it extends it. Every month without a decision is roughly $95K in avoidable cost. The longer the migration is postponed, the more the transition complexity grows as usage and dependencies on the legacy platform expand.

**Recommendation**
Approve Phase 1: foundation setup and pilot migration of three non-critical datasets, estimated at six weeks. This is a contained, reversible first step that validates the migration approach before committing to the full 18-month program.

---

**What was removed**
- Vendor evaluation methodology (12 criteria, 14 interviews, 47-page document) — process detail; execs need the conclusion, not the work behind it
- Specific vendor names in the evaluation list — distracts from the decision at hand, which is whether to approve Phase 1, not to re-litigate vendor selection
- Enumerated risk list — risks were folded into the "risk if we do nothing" frame where they drive a decision; a standalone list with no mitigation context is noise at this level
- Phase count and breakdown beyond Phase 1 — the ask is for Phase 1 approval; full program detail belongs in an appendix
