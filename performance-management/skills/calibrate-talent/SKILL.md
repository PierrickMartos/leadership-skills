# Calibrate Talent

Prepare for a calibration meeting with evidence-backed ratings for each direct report.
Calibration is a fairness tool — its purpose is to ensure consistent standards across
the organization, not to rank people against each other.

Every rating you bring must be defensible with specific examples. Gut feelings don't
survive a calibration room.

---

## Step 1: Gather context

### a. Employees

Ask:

> "Who are you calibrating? List each person with their role and level."

You can calibrate one person at a time or the full team. For a full team calibration,
process each person individually, then review the set together in Step 5.

### b. Calibration framework

Ask:

> "Does your company use a specific talent grid or calibration framework?
> If so, share it. If not, I'll use the standard 9-box grid (Performance × Potential)."

**Default: 9-box talent grid**

|  | Low Performance | Solid Performance | High Performance |
|--|----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **High Potential** | Misaligned / New Hire | Growth Employee | Key Talent |
| **Steady Potential** | Variable Contributor | Core Employee | High-Impact Performer |
| **Limited Advancement** | Capability Gap | Effective Contributor | Trusted Professional |

The manager can customize labels to match their company's terminology.

### c. Company values

Ask:

> "What are your company's core values? These will be used to assess values alignment
> as part of the performance rating."

If none provided, assess performance on business results and behaviors only.

### d. Rating scale

Ask:

> "What are your company's performance and potential rating labels?"

**Defaults:**

Performance:
- **Outperformed** — Results beyond goals, exceptional value, benchmark for peers
- **Delivered** — Solid, consistent performance meeting expectations, reliable, high quality
- **Inconsistent** — Variable quality, gaps in meeting responsibilities

Potential:
- **High Potential** — Can move up several levels; strong ambition, quick learner, strategic thinking, emotional intelligence
- **Steady Potential** — Capable of next level; interested in development but not relentlessly ambitious; competent learner
- **Limited Advancement Scope** — Limited capacity or willingness to move beyond current level; may be satisfied with current role

### e. Evidence per employee

For each person, ask:

> "What evidence do you have? (Performance review, 1:1 notes, metrics, peer feedback,
> your own observations)"

## Step 2: Assess performance (per employee)

For each employee, walk through three dimensions:

### a. Business results

- What are the key initiatives they delivered and what were the outcomes?
- How would you rate their overall contribution to business strategy or execution?
- Did they meet the goals set at the start of the period?

**Require specific examples.** "They delivered well" is not enough. What did they deliver? What was the measurable outcome?

### b. Values and behaviors

- To what extent do they align with company values? (Use the values from Step 1c)
- How would you describe their influence as a role model?
- Do they consistently deliver on commitments? (Walk the talk)

### c. Collaboration and team contribution

- How do they contribute to team dynamics?
- How have they supported or collaborated with others to achieve shared goals?
- Do they lift others up, or operate in isolation?

### d. Recommend performance rating

Based on the three dimensions, recommend a rating. **State the specific evidence** that
justifies it. The evidence should be strong enough to defend in a calibration meeting.

> **Format per employee:**
>
> **[Name]** — Performance: **[Rating]**
>
> *Key evidence:*
> - [Specific achievement or behavior with outcome]
> - [Specific achievement or behavior with outcome]
> - [Specific gap or concern, if any]

---

## Step 3: Assess potential (per employee)

For each employee, walk through three dimensions:

### a. Aspiration

- What level of desire have they shown to take on more work or broaden their impact?
- Have they expressed interest in more senior roles or new challenges?
- Do they seek out stretch opportunities, or wait to be assigned?

### b. Learning agility

- How quickly do they learn and apply new skills compared to peers?
- Do they learn from both successes and failures?
- How do they adapt when the situation or requirements change?

### c. Engagement

- How often do they exert discretionary effort — going beyond what's required?
- What is their level of commitment as shown through actions (not just words)?

### d. Recommend potential rating

Based on the three dimensions, recommend a rating with evidence.

> **Format per employee:**
>
> **[Name]** — Potential: **[Rating]**
>
> *Key evidence:*
> - [Specific indicator of aspiration, learning agility, or engagement]
> - [Specific indicator]

---

## Step 4: Plot on the grid

For each employee, combine performance and potential ratings to place them on the talent grid.

For each grid position, note the recommended actions:

| Position | Recommended Actions |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Key Talent** (High Performance + High Potential) | Keep engaged and challenged. Assign to strategic projects. High flight risk — take direct action to retain. |
| **High-Impact Performer** (High Performance + Steady Potential) | Recognize contributions. Deepen expertise. May not want to move up — and that's valuable. |
| **Trusted Professional** (High Performance + Limited Advancement) | Acknowledge as a pillar of the team. Leverage their expertise. Don't push unwanted growth. |
| **Growth Employee** (Solid Performance + High Potential) | Invest in development. Provide stretch assignments. Remove blockers to performance. |
| **Core Employee** (Solid Performance + Steady Potential) | The backbone of the team. Ensure they feel valued. Offer development at their pace. |
| **Effective Contributor** (Solid Performance + Limited Advancement) | Stable contributor. Ensure role fit. Recognize consistency. |
| **Misaligned / New Hire** (Low Performance + High Potential) | Diagnose misalignment. Wrong role? Wrong team? Insufficient support? Act quickly — potential without performance is a signal, not a pass. |
| **Variable Contributor** (Low Performance + Steady Potential) | Address performance gaps directly. Set clear expectations with timelines. |
| **Capability Gap** (Low Performance + Limited Advancement) | Have an honest conversation. Is this the right role? Is improvement realistic? |

## Step 5: Bias and consistency check

**This step is critical.** Review all ratings together before finalizing.

### a. Distribution check

Look at the overall distribution:
- Is everyone rated "Delivered"? → **Central tendency bias.** Re-read the rating definitions. Differentiation is part of the job.
- Is everyone "High Potential"? → Recalibrate against the definitions. High Potential means several levels of advancement — that's genuinely rare.
- Is the distribution consistent with what you'd expect for a team this size?

### b. Per-employee bias check

For each employee, ask:

| Bias | Check |
|------|-------|
| **Recency** | Am I overweighting the last few weeks? Does my evidence span the full period? |
| **Horns / Halo** | Is one event (positive or negative) coloring everything? Does each dimension have independent evidence? |
| **Similarity** | Am I rating this person higher because they think or work like me? |
| **Contrasting** | Am I comparing this person to a teammate instead of to the role expectations? Each person should be rated against their level's expectations, not against each other. |
| **Strictness / Leniency** | Would I apply the same standard to someone else at this level with this output? |

### c. Consistency check

- Are two people at the same level rated differently for similar output? Why?
- Are two people rated the same despite meaningfully different output? Why?
- Would you be comfortable explaining every rating to HR, to the employee, and to their peers?

Flag and adjust any ratings where bias or inconsistency is detected.

For each employee, produce:

>
> **Performance:** [Rating] | **Potential:** [Rating] | **Grid Position:** [Position name]
>
> **Performance evidence:**
> - [Key evidence point]
> - [Key evidence point]
>
> **Potential evidence:**
> - [Key evidence point]
> - [Key evidence point]

> - [From grid position recommendations]
>

## Tone

- Honest and direct. Calibration is not the time for diplomatic hedging.
- Evidence-first. Every rating statement should be followed by "because..." with a specific example.
- Fair. The same standards applied to everyone at the same level.
- Practical. The output must be usable in a calibration meeting — scannable, defensible, specific.
- Calibration is about improving fairness across the organization. Approach it with that purpose.
